What is Shahnameh?

An epic a thousand years in the making.

Composed around the year 1010 by the poet Abolqasem Ferdowsi, the Shahnameh — the “Book of Kings” — is the national epic of Iran: 50,000 couplets recounting the mythic, heroic, and historic past of the Persian world.

Mythology, heroes, and history — playable.

For the first time, the entire 50-level arc of Ferdowsi’s epic is being adapted as a free, mobile-native RPG. Read each tale, fight its monsters, recruit its heroes, and unlock the next legend.

Meet Ferdowsi

The poet who saved a civilization with verse.

For three decades Ferdowsi labored in poverty, refusing to let the language and memory of Persia be lost. He gave us the Shahnameh — a single, towering work that has shaped Persian identity for a thousand years.

“I built with my poetry a palace so high,
that the wind and the rain shall never bring it down.” — Ferdowsi, Shahnameh

In our game, Ferdowsi is your narrator. He opens each chapter, walks you through its meaning, and rewards you for finishing the verse.
