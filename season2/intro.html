A · The Poet

Ferdowsi of Tus

فردوسیِ توس

بِه نامِ خداوندِ جان و خِرَد
کزین برتر اندیشه برنگذرد

One man · thirty years · sixty thousand verses.

When the empires of Persia had been broken and her language was bending toward forgetfulness, a poet from Tus picked up a pen and began the slow work of remembering. Ferdowsi did not invent the kings he named. He carried them across the ruined ground so that a child a thousand years later could still say, in her own tongue, the words for dawn and throne and fire.

What he saved was not a king-list. He saved a way of being Iranian.

"I shall not die, for I am alive — for I have sown the seed of words."

B · The Book

The Shahnameh

شاهنامه — کتابِ شاهان

The Book of Kings.

A single epic that holds the whole memory of Persia: dynasty after dynasty, hero after hero, demon after demon, from the first king of the dawn to the last battle of the age. It is history dressed as myth, and myth dressed as history. They cannot be separated and you are not meant to.

The Shahnameh is the preservation of Persian identity itself — language, ritual, calendar, courage, sorrow. Where empires fell, the verses did not. Where the language of the court changed, Ferdowsi's Persian held. A nation's mythology, kept alive by one poet's stubbornness.

C · The Age

The Age of Kings Begins

آغازِ روزگارِ شاهان

Before the dynasties of Iran, before the Achaemenids, before the prophet of fire — the Pishdadians.

The first dynasty of the Shahnameh is older than memory: a line of kings who taught humans to bind hair and hand fire and split iron. They are called Pishdadi — those who set the foremost law. The chronicle begins with the very first of them.

D · The World Before

A World Without Roof

جهانی بی‌سقف

No fire. No alphabet. No measure of time.

The valleys were full of teeth. The forests were older than language. Children slept under leaves and woke under hawks. There was no single word the people had in common. There was no shared night, no shared morning.

And then — high up, where only goats and weather went — a man stood on the cold rock and looked down.

E · The Two

Light against Darkness

روشنی در برابرِ تاریکی

☀

Ahura · Light

Order. Speech. Fire kept. Memory. Kings.

☾

Ahriman · Dark

Forgetting. Hunger. The shape under the leaf.

The Shahnameh does not pretend they are equal. It pretends they are old.

F · The First King

Keyumars Rises

برخاستنِ کیومرث

On a high crag of ancient Pars, the first man takes a leopard's skin across his shoulders.

He has no army yet. He has no city. He has only the wind, the firelight, and the silence after he speaks. The lions come down from the high pasture and lie at his feet. The mountain learns his name.

This is where your chronicle begins.
